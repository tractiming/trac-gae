During the Workout: The athletes began the workout running as they normally would. Each time the runner passes the reader a split time is recorded. The times are then updated on the smartphone app. The coach can then provide live feedback to the athletes. For example Jamison and Michael ran 85 sec for the first 400 m, which was too slow for their goal pace. With feedback provided by the app, the runners were able to speed up and reach their goal pace.

"By taking the work associated with splits out of the equation entirely, I can focus on actually watching the athletes and giving constructive feedback in real time." Coach Peter Foote

After the Workout: The coach or athlete can log onto the website to view a deeper analysis of the data obtained during the workout. From the live graph, you can see the various splits over time and the toggle functionality allows you to compare runners head-to-head. This allows the coach to gain valuable insights into his athleteâ€™s performance. For instance, all four athletes were able to maintain consistent performance through the first two intervals. On the last interval as the athletes were tiring some were able to drop to a faster pace while others slowed down. This enables the coach to effectively choose future workouts and set individualized paces and workout goals.
